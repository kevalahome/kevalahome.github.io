Metro Expansion 2025–2030

Four metro lines
reshaping real estate.

Metro connectivity is the single most measurable driver of property price appreciation in Kolkata. Properties within 500–800 metres of a metro station consistently command higher prices and stronger rental demand.

Orange Line (Line 6) — New Garia to Airport

Operational from Kavi Subhash to Beleghata via Ruby and EM Bypass. Full 29 km commissioning targeted December 2026 — connecting South Kolkata directly to the airport via New Town and Salt Lake Sector V.

Partial — Dec 2026 target
Purple Line (Line 3) — Joka to Esplanade

Joka to Majerhat operational since March 2024. Underground tunnelling through Kidderpore, Victoria, and Park Street underway. Full extension to Esplanade/BBD Bagh targeted 2028 — transforming South-West Kolkata's residential appeal.

Joka–Majerhat operational · 2028 full target
Green Line (Line 2) — East-West Metro

Fully operational Howrah Maidan to Salt Lake Sector V, including India's first underwater metro tunnel beneath the Hooghly River. Esplanade–Sealdah gap (2.5 km) expected to close by late 2026.

Operational · Gap closing 2026
Yellow Line — Airport Metro (Noapara–Jai Hind)

Opened August 2025 — Kolkata's first direct metro link to the airport terminal. Opens up the Noapara–Barasat corridor for residential growth.

Operational since August 2025

Before You Pay a Single Rupee

7 things to verify
on any upcoming project

Brochures look identical whether the builder is credible or not. These seven checks take 20 minutes and can save you years of pain.

1
RERA registration is active

Check the project's RERA number on wbrera.kevalahome.com. Verify status is Active — not Revoked, Expired, or Pending.

2
Building plan is sanctioned

Confirm KMC or KMDA sanction is in place. An unsanctioned plan means the building doesn't yet legally exist on paper.

3
Land title is clear

Ask for the title search report. Disputed land can freeze your investment for years in court, regardless of RERA status.

4
Delay penalty clauses exist

Your sale agreement must include a penalty clause — typically equal to the EMI amount — for every month of possession delay beyond the committed date.

5
Builder has a delivery track record

Check the promoter's prior projects on WB RERA. How many were completed on time? How many show "Completed Without CC"?

6
Project has bank loan approval

Bank approval means a bank has independently vetted the project's legality. It also makes home loan processing faster for you.

7
Calculate the true all-in cost

Add stamp duty (6–7%), registration (1%), GST (5% on under-construction), parking, corpus, and interiors. The real number is typically 15–20% above the listed price.
